VAT Reconciliation — Sage vs SARS
Resolved

A period-by-period reconciliation of the Sage VAT control account against the SARS eFiling ledger was completed, isolating and clearing historical discrepancies accumulated across multiple filing periods.

Sage and SARS VAT positions are now materially aligned across all prior periods.
Root cause identified: timing differences and a Lenhle Quantity Surveyors credit note posted to a nil-return period rather than the correct VAT period.
The Lenhle credit note will be carried to the correct period in the 202604 VAT return.
The 202604 VAT return (due late June 2026) will be the first filing to capture the GDID settlement output VAT. This is a material liability and requires close review before submission.
Payroll — Lerato Sefoka
Resolved

A multi-period payroll discrepancy was identified, investigated, and fully resolved. Root cause: Sage Payroll's cumulative PAYE recalculation after a salary reduction generated negative PAYE added to net pay, inflating take-home across several months while EMP201s were filed with positive PAYE figures.

Root cause fully understood, documented, and remediated on a roll-forward basis — no edits to historical payroll periods.
Lerato's payroll regime restored to a fixed monthly salary, consistent with the historical arrangement prior to the discrepancy period.
An annual bonus was provisioned and processed in recognition of Lerato's personal sacrifices and contribution to the business during a difficult period.
Personal payments made by Maps on Maru's behalf during the discrepancy period have been flagged for credit to the Maps loan account.
Over-remittance position to be resolved through the EMP501 annual reconciliation with Junior.

Bongolethu JPS Contract Review — DEET
Action required

The CIDB SLA and addendum issued by the Gauteng Department of Employment and Tourism for the Bongolethu JPS project were reviewed in full. Five material risk clauses were identified, assessed, and documented.

Double-recovery penalty: exposes Maru Spaces to paying twice for the same alleged shortfall with no performance appeal mechanism.
PI insurance cession to DEET: transferring PI policy rights to a client is non-standard and potentially uninsurable — Aon must be consulted via the LRMS service (Sam Varela / Siva Naidoo) before any response is issued.
Open-ended retention and 10-year liability tail: both disproportionate to a professional services agreement at standard fee levels.
Fee linkage to SACAP-conflicting conditions: creates a regulatory conflict that cannot be resolved without SACAP's explicit approval.
Do not sign this contract as-is. A written objection prioritising the three highest-risk clauses should be submitted before the deadline.
Executive Compensation Modelling
Resolved

Following receipt of the GDID settlement funds, a financial model was constructed to assess available director extraction routes — dividend declaration, salary, and director's loan account repayment — against their respective tax, timing, and compliance consequences.

Net retained cash and net VAT payable to SARS calculated post-distribution to sub-consultants, legal costs, and the Pholosang facilitation fee.
Modelling concluded with an 80/20 salary/dividend split as the optimal extraction structure.
Mapula's April remuneration was captured and processed within the May payroll cycle, with backpay for April accommodated.
Repayment of the existing director's loan balance (R57,992.99 credit in Maps' favour) confirmed as a tax-free repayment event, separate from the extraction structure.
PI Insurance — Aon Premium Review
Resolved

An error in Aon's calculation of Maru Spaces' professional indemnity insurance premium was identified and addressed directly with the broker.

The basis of the fee income declaration on the proposal form was identified as incorrect — the GDID litigation settlement receipt had been treated as professional fee income, which it is not.
The distinction between settlement proceeds and remuneration for work undertaken was communicated to Aon to ensure the declaration accurately reflects Maru's actual fee income.
Correction ensures no disclosure risk at claim stage under the iToo Special Risks (Hollard) policy.

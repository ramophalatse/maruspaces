User Guide

Project Profitability Tracker

Maru Spaces (Pty) Ltd  ·  Confidential  ·  v1.0 · 14 May 2026

This tool helps you price a project before you quote, set a cost budget, and track whether you're spending inside it as the project runs.

The tool works in any browser. Your data is stored securely in the cloud — you can log in from any device and pick up where you left off.

Before you begin

You'll need your login credentials. If you haven't signed in before, use the Create one link on the sign-in screen. Once your account is created, all projects you create are shared across the Maru Spaces workspace.

01

The four tabs

Every project has four tabs. They're designed to be worked through in order when setting up a new project, and then revisited as work progresses.

Tab 01
Setup
Enter the project fee, stage splits, staff rates, and sub-consultant budget. These are the inputs that drive everything else.
Tab 02
Budget
Derived automatically from Setup. Shows your planned cost per stage, budgeted hours per staff category, and planned gross margin.
Tab 03
Actuals
Log actual hours worked per stage as the project runs. Sub-consultant spend is entered in rands. Variance against budget updates live.
Tab 04
Summary
Your project health dashboard. Planned margin vs forecast margin, stage progress bars, and a full cost breakdown at a glance.
02

Setting up a new project

Click + New Project in the left sidebar to get started. You'll be asked for a project name, number, client, and fee value. These can all be edited later from the Setup tab.

1
Enter the project fee
Enter the contracted fee excluding VAT. This is the total amount Maru will invoice the client for professional services — excluding disbursements.
2
Set the production cost percentage
The default is 45%, implying a 55% gross margin. Adjust this if the project complexity warrants a different cost assumption. The implied margin updates live as you type.
3
Review staff rates
Your Partners rate is a manual input. For Associates, Architectural Staff, and Staff under Supervision, rates are calculated from annual salary and a charge-out multiplier. The default multiplier is 2.5×. Adjust salaries or multipliers to reflect your actual team costs for this project.
4
Set the stage fee splits
Each of the seven SACAP stages gets a percentage of the total fee. These must sum to 100%. Default splits are pre-loaded — adjust them to match your fee schedule if it differs.
5
Add sub-consultant budget (if applicable)
If you're engaging sub-consultants on this project, enter their budgeted cost per stage. Use the Rate × Hours calculator to compute amounts from a rate and estimated hours, or enter rand amounts directly. Sub-consultant costs sit inside the production budget and reduce your margin.
6
Review the staff mix
Each stage allocates its staff budget across the four categories according to the mix percentages. The default mix reflects a typical architecture project. Adjust if you know certain stages will be more senior-heavy or more junior-heavy than the default.
Auto-save

Every change saves automatically to the cloud. You'll see a brief ● Saved confirmation in the top right corner. There is no save button — just make the change and move on.

03

Logging actuals

Once a project is underway, the Actuals tab is where you record what's actually being spent. The discipline of logging hours regularly is what gives the tool its value — without actuals, the Summary tab can only show you the plan.

Staff hours

For each stage, enter the cumulative hours worked by each staff category. These are running totals — not weekly increments. Update them as often as makes sense for the project, ideally at least once a month.

Sub-consultant spend

Enter the actual rand amount invoiced by sub-consultants per stage. This updates as invoices are received and approved.

Reading the variance

Each row shows Budget, Actual, and Variance. Variance is calculated as Budget minus Actual — a positive number means you are under budget, a negative number means you have overrun.

What to watch

Stage 5 — construction supervision — typically carries the highest risk of overrun on projects with long build programmes. Log hours here consistently and watch the variance column. If it turns negative early, flag it before the overrun compounds.

04

Reading the Summary

The Summary tab gives you a live read on project health. It updates automatically as you log actuals.

The four headline numbers

Fee Value
The contracted fee excl. VAT.
Planned Margin
What the margin should be if costs stay within the production budget assumption.
Forecast Margin
What the margin will be based on costs incurred to date. This is the number to watch — it tells you whether the project is tracking to plan.
Budget Consumed
What percentage of the production budget has been spent. At 80% consumed with significant work still to do, the project needs attention.

Project status indicator

Each project shows a status pill based on the forecast margin:

On track — forecast margin above 45%
Watch — forecast margin 30–45%
At risk — forecast margin below 30%

Stage progress bars

Each stage shows a progress bar indicating how much of its cost budget has been consumed. Bars turn amber above 80% and red above 100%. A stage that has consumed its full budget but is not yet complete is a margin risk.

05

A few things to know

Disbursements are not tracked here

Travel costs, printing, and other disbursements are billed to clients on top of fees as a pass-through. They do not affect your margin and are not entered into this tool. Track them separately in your invoicing records.

The tool is shared

Any project you create is visible to all authorised users of the Maru Spaces workspace. There is no private mode. Keep project names and numbers consistent with your office filing system so they're easy to find.

Assumptions are per-project

Stage splits, staff mix, rates, and cost percentage are all set independently for each project. Changing assumptions on one project does not affect any other. Default values are pre-loaded as a starting point — always review them before treating the Budget tab as final.

The budget is a plan, not a constraint

The tool shows you what the plan was and how actuals compare. It does not prevent you from logging hours that exceed budget. It is a visibility tool — the decisions remain yours.
